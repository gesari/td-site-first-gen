Azaian Magic

While slightly a misnomer, given the azonian nature of spell architecture, the traditional term for spellcraft is Azaian Magic. Since their bodies are made up of azonian structures, dreamers and other sentient entities are capable of channeling azai through their bodies to magical effect. Dreamers have three ways of wielding azai: their innate psionics, spell architecture, and free casting.

The Azonai

Magic, or the ability to interact and/or manipulate the Dream via non-physical means, is a very natural process that has its basis in the very fundament of the Dreamscape itself. The Azonai is the Source of the Dream, from which all dimensions and phenomena arise, but it is a Source that is inferred rather than observed. More accessible are its twin manifestations of azon (static essence) and azai (flowing essence).

Azon

All physical phenomena are composed of azonian structures: a stable substance which allows Azonai to manifest as physical matter. It is also used by dreamers in the crafting of spells: their architectures are formed with azonian circuitry which channels the flow of azai through them to create consistently reproducible results.

Azai

The entirety of the Dreamscape is permeated with azai, most notably in the form of the Azaian Flow: stellar aurorae which emerge from and travel between stars, bathing all celestial bodies near them in azaian energies. The flowing essence of the Dream fills and powers azonian structures to create emergent phenomena such as life and spellcraft.

Spell Architecture

Architecture is the term given to spells that follow traditional crafting conventions, all of which are engineered around safe, efficient casting. Their effects are reliable and repeatable, but general enough that they will adapt to many situations based on the creativity of the caster. Their manifestation is likewise guided by the user's Attunement and Style, and the overall design is efficient enough to require minimal amounts of azai relative to the size of the effect produced. As such, casters are generally able to employ spell architecture for long periods of time without tiring.

Spell architectures are learned processes, and are considered Skills. PCs will learn spell architectures from the college associated with their specialization automatically, but can train in additional colleges of magic to diversify their abilities if they so choose. Individual spells can be learned for any reason that may fit the game's narrative, so long as their prerequisites have been met.

A full list of spell architectures, their Spell Tiers, and other prerequisites are listed in the Spell Architecture Catalogue.

Spell Tiers

A dreamer's attainment of a Spell Tier corresponds to the complexity of magics they are able to wield. All spell architectures correspond to one of the first three Spell Tiers, which serves as a prerequisite for their acquisition. While dreamers are capable of attempting to Free-Cast spells of a higher Tier, it is at a severe disadvantage and generally discouraged. Higher Spell Tiers correspond to the magical feats associated with dreamers of such achievement. Greater details on the benefits of each Spell Tier are included in the Dreamers section, but have been noted again here to draw a correlation between those Tiers and the different classifications of spells.

The Three Schools and Nine Colleges of Azaian Magic

There are three schools of magic, which correlate to the fundamental (Unification), physical (Projection), and emergent (Adaptation) aspects of the Dreamscape; each of these schools of magic has three corresponding colleges, for a total of nine. Learning spells will come from study of individual colleges. Characters automatically study in the college which correlates to their chosen specialization, though others can be pursued in tandem if they desire.

The School of Adaptation

Adaptation Magic relates to the emergent systems of the Dream that arise from the interactivity of azon and azai. The circulating currents of the Azaian Flow, the evolution of inorganic matter into life, and the emergence of consciousness all fall under its purview.

The College of Animism

All physical phenomena within the Dream are connected by subtle threads, near-imperceptible currents of azai that reach all the way back to the Flow. Study of the source of these threads, and the ability to manipulate them, falls to the students of the Animism College. Thoughts and minds can be merged, spirits may be communed with, and memories altered through its spells.

The College of Harmonics

The systems which Occlusion magic seeks to preserve each require a unique balance of their own, which is promoted by Harmonic magic. Study of Harmonics grants a caster great insight into living beings and their souls, allowing for great capacity to heal and harm.

The College of Occlusion

Emergent systems tend to require very specific systems to be kept in balance in order to function and flourish. Preservation of this balance falls under the purview of Occlusion magics. Typical examples include shielding spells and the creation of defensive barriers.

The School of Projection

Projection magics deal with the physical manifestation of the Dream and its purposeful alteration. Explosive displays of power, enchanting illusory murals, and the manufacture of magical items and arcanomatons are all made possible through its pursuit.

The College of Conduction

The Dream is everywhere suffused with vast amounts of ambient azai. Students of Conduction magic learn to direct its flow, effectively creating their own currents of azaian energies. These methods can be used to generate and distribute vast amounts of power, but can just as easily be turned to destructive ends.

The College of Enchantment

The College of Enchantment deals with the modification of physical objects. It can help make someone stronger, imbue items with permanent enchantments, and even be employed to create constructs.

The College of Perception

Spells which alter one's perception of the Dream come from the Perception subschool. They can be used to enhance one's appearance, turn invisible, and even trick an entity into believing it is somewhere, or someone, it is not.

The School of Unification

Unification magics focus on the underlying properties of things, and are called such in reference to Thymoran Unified Theory: a scholarly tradition relating to the interconnected nature of all different implementations of azon and azai within the Dream. Through the Colleges of Unification magic, all things within the Dream can be directly related to one another.

The College of Augury

The Augury College focuses on scrying magics in an attempt to perceive the Dream in all its facets. Its study can allow a caster to see faraway locations, or even astrally project their consciousness to them. Through study and contemplation of the Oracle, the dimension of swirling possibility which contains echoes of both the past and potential futures, they may even see into, and affect, the minds of other dreamers.

The College of Translocation

The bending of space is the primary purpose of Translocation magics. As a mutable dimension, the distance between two points within the Dream is not necessarily static, allowing casters to traverse vast distances in a single step, or to do so locally, giving casters access to seemingly supernatural speeds.

The College of Transmutation

Originally studied as the esoteric art of alchemy, Transmutation magics alter the fundamental nature of things. Typical uses are for shapeshifting, altering the dimensions or properties of an object, and reshaping the environment.

Free-Casting

When a dreamer casts a spell without using specifically engineered architecture and instead seeks to improvise one on the spot, the process is much less efficient, and winds up costing the dreamer some of their excess azai. In the mechanics of the game, this means it drains their Shield; it also means that a dreamer must have available Shield points to spend if they wish to Free-Cast a spell. If they do not, they must use a Recharge action or other means of restoring Shield value before casting the intended spell. Additionally, spells which are Free-Cast have a chance of going awry, which can cause some unintended side effects, not all of which are desirable, and almost none of which are within the caster's control. That being said, Free-Casting spells is a very versatile form of casting, as it affords a dreamer the ability to do virtually anything within their capabilities, so long as they are careful.

Dreamers that have achieved the Fourth Tier may Free-Cast effects together as a group, amplifying the spell's Potency and chance of success.

Shield Cost

Every Free-Cast action is powered by the caster’s internal reserves of azai, represented by expending the caster’s available Shield value; the specific cost equates to the Potency of the spell being cast. Casters may elect any even number between 2 and their max Potency as the value for any Free-Cast action they take: the value must be even so that it may be neatly halved in the event of a Partial Success. This cost is incurred regardless of the action's Degree of Success.

Free-Casting Penalties

As they are inherently harder to cast, all Free-Cast attempts carry a basic difficulty penalty that is determined in relation to both the Spell Tier of the caster and the Tier of spell being cast. Because of the rather open nature of Free-Casting, the Spell Tier and Colleges will be determined by the GM when a spell is attempted. This will determine not only the action's base modifier, but also which of the dreamer's own stat modifiers apply.

Free-Casting Degrees of Success

Due to the volatility of Free-Cast spells, there are certain additional effects that happen based on the spell's Degree of Success.

Falter The spell's architecture collapses and creates instability in the local azaian currents. The dreamer rolls on the Vortex Table to determine the effects.
Failure The spell is unable to take effect, but no additional effects are triggered.
Partial Success The spell is cast at half Potency, as the dreamer is not able to skillfully execute it. The dreamer rolls on the Surge Table to determine the effects.
Full Success The spell is cast at full Potency as intended.
Plus The dreamer is able to execute the spell with a flourish. They may choose any effect from the Surge Table to modify the spell's effect.

Surge Table

Even when a spell goes off, sometimes it doesn't always happen according to plan. Sometimes a dreamer will lose focus momentarily and fumble some of the spell's construction, or sometimes they pull it off so perfectly that it exceeds their initial expectations. In those instances, the Surge Table will add complications of varying degrees. Some are good, some are bad, and some depend on the situation.

1 Caster must roll on Surge Table for all spells, even architectures, until they are able to Short Rest.
2 Caster becomes petrified and will slowly "unthaw" over the course of a Long Rest.
3 Caster's Focus becomes magically inert until targeted with a Recharge ability that achieves a Plus degree.
4 Casting the spell drains the caster’s remaining Shield.
5 The spell’s Shield cost is doubled. If the caster does not have enough Shield value to cover the additional cost, the spell fails and they are still charged for the action.
6 The caster fuses to whatever surface they are currently on. If flying, they are surrounded by a cushion of air and unable to move. They receive 10 stacks of Immobilized.
7 The spell's base Potency becomes 0.
8 The spell's effect is reversed, or rebounds.
9 The Spell's check is rerolled, but its Degree of Success is lowered by 1 after the new result has been calculated.
10 The spell strikes a resonant chord with the spiritsong: ethereal sounds seem to follow the caster until their next Long Rest.
11 The caster swaps their location with the spell's target.
12 The local area is transfigured according to the caster's personal aesthetic.
13 The caster is partially astrally projected until a Long Rest: they may control themselves as normal, but view everything with a Third-Person perspective. Their Cognition and Force bonuses are halved, rounded down, during this time, unless Insight is their highest of the three stats, in which case Caster's Cognition bonus is also unaffected and instead added to their Force bonus.
14 The caster's Shield cost for Free-Casting this spell is refunded.
15 The spell's base Potency is doubled.
16 The caster receives a static bonus of +10 for checks until the end of next Round.
17 Damage dealt by the caster heals them until their next Short Rest.
18 The target is suffused with azai and begins to glow from within. For the rest of the Round, their actions, and actions targeting them, have +1 Polarity to both dice pools.
19 The caster's Shield value can not be reduced below 1 until the next Long Rest; damage that would reduce the caster's Shield below 1 will instead be applied to their Vitality. Free-casting with 1 Shield does not incur any costs.
20 The action and Shield expended to cast this spell are refunded, and the caster’s next action has a guaranteed Plus degree.

Vortex Table

An Azaian Vortex is a storm of magical power which begins to unravel and then disintegrate all azonian structures they contact. A small one might devour a neighborhood and all its inhabitants before the Flow is restored, but truly large ones are capable of devouring entire realms. One of the most famous vortex sites is on the satellite Keshal, where a vortex left a hole in the surface through which the light of stars on the far side of the moon can still be easily glimpsed over two millennia later.

To effectively quell a Vortex, it must be infused with enough azai to effectively render it inert. In terms of the game, this requires an action with a Potency value equal to or greater than the Vortex’s current Potency value. If at least one actor attempting to quell a Vortex has attained the Fourth Spell Tier or higher, they can combine Potency values across individuals for the purpose of meeting this requirement.

1 A Vortex spawns, centered on the caster; it behaves like Arc Vortex with a guaranteed Plus Degree and base Potency of 10. The Vortex radius expands by 1 unit per turn it is sustained. While the Vortex is maintained, it consistently drains the Caster of their magical energy regardless of their location, afflicting them with the Status Effect: Suppressed. It behaves as a Channel spell the caster cannot consciously terminate, with base maximum Polarity on all Channeling checks. If the radius of the vortex effectively doubles, it is no longer counted as a Channel and instead has stabilized of its own accord, and will continue growing until quelled.
2 The local area becomes a vacuum of air and azai for 2 rounds. Entities inside take 6 damage on first Round, and 3 on second, from momentary asphyxiation.
3 Everyone in the spell’s range and the caster themself are translocated to the Dark Star Terminus.
4 All of the caster's talismans and sigils are rendered magically inert and must be repaired/reinked.
5 A doppelganger of the caster spawns, with their current stats mirrored as the caster's max. The doppelganger will suffer damage equal to the caster's Potency every Round and seek to kill the Caster in order to take their place until it dies and dissipates; if the caster dies before their doppelganger, the impostor achieves their goal and no longer loses Vitality every Round. The doppelganger will do everything in its power to convince others that they are the real variant.
6 The spell drains any remaining Shields, dealing damage equal to Shield drained plus the Shield value expended to cast the spell, and the caster's Shield cannot be restored before a Long Rest. If the spell used all their remaining Shield, they instead suffer damage equal to their full Potency damage plus the Shield value expended to cast it.
7 An explosion with a 2 unit radius erupts from the caster equal to triple their Potency.
8 A Chain Reaction spell is triggered on the caster with double their Attack/Potency values.
9 Caster is cut off from the Azaian Flow and is Suppressed until they take a Long Rest. This does not count against their Status Effect cap of 2, and their Shield maximum is set to 0 while under this effect.
10 Damage dealt to the caster will heal attackers until their next Short Rest.
11 The caster is given a grisly portent with no immediate way of determining its veracity.
12 The caster's Spell Tier is reduced by 1 until a Long Rest. If they are only at Spell Tier 1, they are restricted to the use of their Innate Psionics.
13 The local area is transfigured to reflect the caster's idea of a worst possible outcome.
14 An explosion with a 4 unit radius erupts from the caster equal to double their Potency.
15 If the spell is targeting an entity, the caster polymorphs into the target, and the target is polymorphed into the caster. Otherwise, the caster instead is polymorphed into a form that is incredibly disadvantageous to the situation. This counts as a Channel spell the caster cannot consciously terminate.
16 The caster is unable to power, or receive benefits from, any of their talismans and/or sigils until their next Short Rest.
17 Everyone in the spell’s range and the caster themself are translocated to the Oracle.
18 Everyone within the spell’s range and the caster themself comes under the effect of the Phase spell with a Partial Success degree. This counts as a Channel spell the caster cannot consciously terminate, and the effect on each entity is tied to the caster's Channel.
19 The caster is unable to invoke Lucidity Tokens until after a Long Rest.
20 The caster may reroll the spell’s check. This check cannot trigger an effect from the Vortex or Surge Table regardless of result.
