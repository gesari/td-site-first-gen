Dreamers

A generic catch-all term for any type of sentient entity within the dream, most dreamers are generally humanoid, though their forms are incredibly varied in shape, size, and traits. Many dreamers have appearances that are either animalistic or elemental in nature, and their form is not necessarily determined by birth. Though they are born as an amalgamation of their parents, many dreamers will find their physical form altered as they mature in accordance with their Attunement, personality, general culture, or some combination thereof.

The Astral Body

All dreamers have a magical component to their body, traditionally referred to as the astral body. The astral body is a natural magical organ which arises from a dreamer's soul and confers upon them the ability to channel azai, both within their own body and the area around them. This fundamental ability gives rise to the twin psionic blessings of telekinesis and telepathy in all dreamers, which serve as the basis for their ability to craft spell architectures. A dreamer's ability to wield their innate abilities progress in tandem with them as they advance through Spell Tiers.

Telekinetics

Telekinetic Hand | First Tier

Dreamers have the ability to interact with objects within range of their astral body. When acting this way, they are able to manipulate objects as if interacting with them with one hand.

Telekinetic Force | Second Tier

As they develop the ability to channel their energies towards and into other entities, dreamers are able to use their Telekinetics to forcefully move things. When used against a hostile entity, this is counted as an attack.

Partial Success Target entity is moved a number of units equal to half Potency.
Full Success Target entity is moved a number of units equal to full Potency.
Plus Target entity is moved a number of units equal to full Potency + 2.

Telepathics

Telepathy | First Tier

Dreamers have the ability to communicate with each other telepathically and understand each other regardless of language within range of their psionics.

Astral Communion | Second Tier

When communicating telepathically, Dreamers can choose to share intimate access with each other's mind, allowing them to share thoughts, feelings, and memories.

Recollection | Third Tier

Upon achieving the third Spell Tier, dreamers can reach out to the spiritsong and attempt to recall the memories specific to a place or object.

Detect Magic

Dreamers have the ability to see and detect magic around them, a skill which is honed as their magical acumen increases.

Spell Tiers

All dreamers have a base range for their psionics, and thus the rest of their magical abilities. These ranges, much like a dreamer's magical acumen, tend to plateau at certain points, hence the term Spell Tiers. Each Spell Tier corresponds to a dreamer's capacity to store azai, specific ability ranges, and the ability to cast specific types of spells. As PCs gain levels, their Spell Tier will increase automatically at specific thresholds, amplifying their range and acumen.

First Spell Tier | Level 1
Base Shield 10
Ability Range 3

First Tier spells and abilities tend only to affect the caster or some point in reach of their limbs. Their efforts have begun to pay off, but there is still much to learn.

Second Spell Tier | Level 3
Base Shield 15
Ability Range 10

Second Tier spells are able to affect targets beyond the caster and manipulate the environment itself, rather than simply interacting with it. This is a potent state of achievement, and where most dreamers find their limit.

Third Spell Tier | Level 5
Base Shield 20
Ability Range 15

Third Tier spells and effects begin to affect the very fabric of the dream and need not be localized, depending on the spell. Dreamers who attain Third Order magic still have much to go before achieving Lucidity, but this achievement is the first that formally marks them as a candidate for ascension.

Fourth Spell Tier | Level 7
Base Shield 25
Ability Range 20

Beyond the Third Spell Tier, the ability range of dreamers begins to plateau, and the benefits become more subtle. The Fourth Tier, or First Awakening, corresponds to subtle mastery of a dreamer's astral body and their manipulation of azai. Dreamers at this stage may shape their spells around entities within their range, allowing them to exclude specific targets from hostile effects. They may also manipulate up to three objects at a time with their Telekinetics.

Fifth Spell Tier | Level 9
Base Shield 30
Ability Range 25

The Fifth Spell Tier, or Second Awakening, coincides with a dreamer's ability to indefinitely maintain Channeled effects, regardless of whether or not they are conscious. Channeled abilities actively sustained by a dreamer persist even when they go to sleep, are knocked out, or are otherwise incapacitated. On a Plus degree, Channel spells no longer require active concentration on the dreamer's part: they function as a permanent enchantment, and endure until the dreamer willfully ends them or they are otherwise terminated.

Sixth Spell Tier | Level 10
Base Shield 35
Ability Range 25

The Sixth Spell Tier is also known as the Final Awakening, and is the point at which a dreamer reaches Lucidity. Though they can still die, they are otherwise immortal. Upon achieving Lucidity, the insight they gain causes all of their collegiate training stats to increase by 1, up to their maximum of 4. If a college is already at its maximum value, this boost instead will confer Magical Adept training if it has not already been studied. If the dreamer has already achieved Magical Adept training, this bonus instead increases their corresponding secondary stat by 1.

Base Statistics

There are three types of statistics: Primary Stats, Secondary Stats, and Collegiate Training. Primary Stats and Collegiate Training are both increased via a Point Buy system: at level 1, Players receive 5 points to spend as they will, and 2 additional points ever level thereafter. Secondary Stats inherit their base values from Primary Stats, and are often enhanced by items and skills.

Vitality

All dreamers have a base Vitality of 6 at level 1. This is indicative of their general hardiness, and it can be actively augmented via stat allocation. Unlike any other Primary stat, Vitality has no associated Secondaries, and a character’s base Vitality increases by 1 per level regardless of additional stat allocations.

Shield

Once a dreamer achieves the first Spell Tier, their capacity to store azai is such that it naturally begins to form a protective barrier around them, which can be enhanced by their unitools. Their base Shield is 10 at the first Spell Tier, and increases in tandem with it, along with any other stat modifiers and/or unitool upgrades the dreamer may benefit from.

Movement

Dreamers have a base movement speed of 5 units. Every Movement Action taken allows them to use this movement at any time during the Round.

Death Mechanics

When a player's Vitality is reduced to 0, they are immediately rendered Unconscious and at risk of dying. When a player is knocked Unconscious, they lose hold on all Channeled and non-permanent effects they are actively sustaining. Their Action Economy becomes 1, and they have no access to Reactions. Until they are stabilized and awaken, they may only use the Stabilize action on themselves once per Round during their Faction Turn; no other actions are available to them. Once Stabilized, an entity's Action Economy is returned to normal and they may act for the remainder of the Round, assuming their condition had not become Unstable prior.

Unconscious | 0 Vitality

When a character is rendered Unconscious, any remaining damage on the ability which rendered them Unconscious is lost: if they suffered 4 damage with only 2 Vitality, they would be reduced to 0 Vitality instead of -2. Further damage dealt to them via additional actions while Unconscious will only subtract a single Vitality on Partial/Full Degrees, or 2 damage in the event of a Plus Degree. If a character's Vitality drops below -2, they die. In the moment of their death, a dreamer may take one final action via their astral body with a guaranteed Plus Degree.

Unstable | -1 Vitality

If an Unconscious player is hit by a hostile action before they have stabilized, they become Unstable. Unstable characters are still able to use one Stabilize action on themselves per Round; on a Success, they revert to Unconscious rather than waking up. Once rendered Unstable, the character cannot awaken until the danger has passed and/or any friendly entities have a moment to tend to/rouse them.

Mortal | -2 Vitality

If an Unstable player is hit by a hostile action before they have stabilized, their wounds become Mortal. They will die at the end of the Round barring some type of intervention that restores them to at least 1 Vitality. Once stabilized, the character will not awaken until they have completed a Long Rest.

Death

If a player's Mortal wounds are not tended to promptly, the player will die. Recently deceased players may be resurrected via a Restore spell.

Stable

When an Unconscious character's Vitality is once again greater than 0, they are considered Stable and have their Action Economy returned as normal. If their condition became Unstable/Mortal before becoming Stable, their Action Economy is not restored (i.e. they won’t wake up) until the effect's conditions are met, but their Vitality may be otherwise restored and they are no longer considered to be in danger.
