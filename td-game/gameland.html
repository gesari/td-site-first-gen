Thymoran Dreams

This is the landing page for the game system, which I have tentatively titled Thymoran Dreams. This section will contain the entire System Codex, as well as a proto-character generator.

Character Generator

The Character Generator is still under construction, but you can still use this part of the site to access the alpha variant made with google sheets! Fully integrated JS version is next on my to-do list.

Degrees of Success

There are five general Degrees of Success, and thus five possible outcomes of any check: Falter, Failure, Partial Success, Success, and Plus. These degrees correspond to specific values, generally from 1 to 20, and are meant to be used as a guideline to determine the result of a check. Once an entity's dice have been rolled and any relevant modifiers have been applied, the result is compared against the degrees of success to determine the effect of their actions.

Actions

Almost everything the Players will do to interact with the game space will be an action of some sort. The four Types of Actions describe the rules which govern the use case of individual actions a Player may take, and the Eight General Actions account for everything a Player might choose to do.

Status Effects

Many, if not most, Skills in the game apply status effects as a component of their usage, typically as a result of a Plus. There are 21 unique status effects in the game, and every entity can suffer the consequences of two distinct effects at a time.

Exploration

There are many miscellaneous details of traveling throughout the game space that don't necessarily fall into other categories, or are small enough as to not warrant one of their own. That data will be stored here.

Dreamers

"Dreamers" is a generic catch-all term for any type of sentient entity within the dream. Most dreamers are generally humanoid, though their forms are incredibly varied in shape, size, and traits. Many dreamers have appearances that are either animalistic or elemental in nature, and their form is not necessarily determined by birth. Though they are born as an amalgamation of their parents, many dreamers will find their physical form altered as they mature in accordance with their Attunement, personality, general culture, or some combination thereof.

Statistics

There are three types of statistics: Primary Stats, Secondary Stats, and Collegiate Training. Primary Stats and Collegiate Training are both increased via a Point Buy system: at level 1, Players receive 5 points to spend as they will, and 2 additional points every level thereafter. Secondary Stats inherit their base values from Primary Stats, and are often enhanced by items and skills.

Style

A character's style develops around their preferences for wielding their Azaian Focus. Every dreamer wields a focus, which they acquire at a relatively young age.

Attributes

Unlike Statistics, a character's Attributes confer bonuses that are generally not numerical. Most of them are very open-ended, as their purpose is primarily to provide mechanics which confirm the story a Player is trying to roleplay with a character.

Items

Every magical item wielded by a dreamer is inextricably attuned to them, and must be powered by their own internal reserves of azai in order to function; for most dreamers, the use of their Focus and Unitool, perhaps supplemented by one or two talismans/sigils, is the limits of their ability. For those who have pushed their limits beyond their innate psionics and begun practicing ordered magics, however, the ability to empower further items is within their power.

Azaian Magic

While slightly a misnomer, given the azonian nature of spell architecture, the traditional term for spellcraft is Azaian Magic. Since their bodies are made up of azonian structures, dreamers and other conscious entities are capable of channeling azai through their bodies to magical effect. Dreamers have three ways of wielding azai: their innate psionics, spell architecture, and free casting.

Spell Architecture Catalog

This is an exhaustive list of every spell architecture in the game. Spells are grouped by School and College, and listed in order of ascending Spell Tier.

Specializations

There are 9 specializations in the game, each associated with a particular college of magic. A specialization is not meant to be a total encapsulation of the college they are based upon, but rather a single interpretation of its potential.

Companions

Companion NPCs are entities that accompany dreamers and act in concert with them. This includes an Animist's Warder, an Operative's Turrets, and any constructs and spirits summoned via learned collegiate architectures.
