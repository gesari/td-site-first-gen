Items

There are 4 types of items: Aurion Foci, Unitools, Talismans, and Sigils.

Every character starts with 2 of these items by default: a Focus and a Unitool. Each item is unique to every dreamer: it has taken a form of their choosing, and is uniquely attuned to them. While the form of each item may change at the dreamer's discretion, they will only have one of these items at a time, and generally will keep the same one for life.

Wielding other magical implements such as Talismans and Sigils requires a certain level of aptitude. Every magical item wielded by a dreamer is inextricably attuned to them, and must be powered by their own internal reserves of azai in order to function; for most dreamers, the use of their Focus and Unitool, perhaps supplemented by one or two talismans/sigils, is the limits of their ability. For those who have pushed their limits beyond their innate psionics and begun practicing higher tiered magics, however, the ability to empower further items is within their power. Dreamers are able to sustain a number of talismans/sigils equal to their spell tier.

Aurion Foci

Characters have a single focus, which serves to facilitate their spellcasting and, should the need arise, function as a weapon. Foci are made with specific aurion and onaian cores which allows them to absorb additional energies and enchantments as they age. It is unusual that a dreamer exchanges their foci for another, as the bond forged between dreamer and focus is deeply personal; with age, most foci become at least semi-sentient, an emergent consciousness which is sustained so long as the tether between the focus and dreamer remains.

In combat, some foci may shift their appearance to something befitting the task at hand. Both appearances are often rather malleable, and will be personal to the bond between the focus and dreamer. Their chosen Style will also guide the manner of their manifestation.

Upgrades

Foci function as living symbiotes which grow in tandem with their wielder. Upon achievement of successive Spell Tiers, a foci's base Potency increases by 2. Additionally, foci can "learn" additional functionalities via blessings and powers bestowed upon them over the course of their lifetime, called an Investiture. Such boons are generally bestowed as a reward or consequence of a dreamer's actions by powerful forces, or invested by a particularly skilled artisan.

Investitures

The below list of blessings placed upon a focus is not meant to be exhaustive: Players are encouraged to generate their own blessings when appropriate to fit a given situation. Blessings of any magnitude may be bestowed at any time the GM feels to be appropriate.

  • Secondary Stat Bonuses:
  • +4 Potency Bonus vs targets in Physical Range
  • Grant access to a Status Effect
  • Move target up to 3 units on a Critical Success
  • Lower threshold for Critical Success by 1
  • Reroll one natural 1 result from a character's dice pool once per Round
  • Reroll a failed Channel Check once per Short Rest
  • Create a unique spell effect
  • Allow the focus to maintain an additional Channeled effect on behalf of its wielder
  • Translocate up to half movement on a Plus Degree for Spells/Abilities

Unitools

After a dreamer's focus, nothing shall be so indispensable to them as their unitool. Traditionally worn as a bracelet on either wrist, it has several standard functions: it acts as a communication device, allowing telepathic communication between users across vast distances; it acts as an information retrieval and storage device, capable of projecting holographic displays of data; it allows the user to interact with all magical technologies, and is even able to connect to the Oracle Archives if it is available; it modulates a dreamer's reserves of azai, creating a protective Shield around them; and it can download and implement certain spell architectures, even if the user has not yet learned the spell themselves.

Like foci, unitools scale in power with the dreamers that wear them, but not due to any change in their own capabilities. Instead, their design is modular enough to be nearly universally applicable to all dreamers, and simply draw only enough power from them as is safe and necessary to function. Almost any dreamer may wear a unitool and provide enough power to operate its standard functions, and most are even capable of producing a Shield. Unitools may be configured to bestow up to three unique bonuses that change based on the dreamer's spell tier, and the unitool's current configuration: Shield conversion, Recharge Potency, and Spell Storage.

Unitool Progression

Upon unlocking their first spell tier, a dreamer's internal reserves of azai are large enough that their unitool is capable of producing a Shield. Base Shield value for any dreamer at the first spell tier is 10, increasing by 5 per additional spell tier reached. When dreamers attain spell tiers beyond the first, their unitool unlocks new functionalities via configuration points which may be allocated to the dreamer's specifications. Each of the three functions which may be enhanced -- Shield conversion, Recharge Potency, and Spell Storage -- have a maximum of three tiers which scale in a relatively linear fashion. Dreamers are capable of changing their configuration as a part of any Long Rest.Sm

Talismans and Sigils

Talismans and Sigils are similar in function, but not necessarily in form. Like unitools, talismans and sigils both rely upon the dreamer that bears them as a battery. Talismans are freely traded magical items which are rendered inert when not attuned to a specific dreamer; the enchantments bestowed upon talismans are permanent, but dormant without a dreamer or some other form of functional, renewable energy source. Sigils, however, are magical tattoos which cannot be deactivated once applied.

While dreamers may have as many talismans as they wish to accumulate and carry, they are limited in how many active talismans and/or sigils they may bear simultaneously, with a cap equal to their spell level. Sigils cannot be deactivated once applied unless permanently removed, and so will always count against this cap, whereas talismans may be activated or not depending on a dreamer's preference, and their attunement may be reconfigured as part of a Short or Long Rest. Unitools and Aurion Foci do not count against this cap.

Talismans

Talismans come in all shapes and sizes, and is a catch-all phrase for any type of magic item/enchanted object. They generally provide unique utility effects which are generally less adaptable, but more reliable, than traditional spell architectures.

Below is a list of Talismans within the game:

Afterimage Treads

These boots bear a unique enchantment which warps any indicators of the bearer's passing. Physical tracks they leave appear to be made by an entirely different dreamer, and any attempt to scry on them will show the bearer and their surroundings, but with an altered appearance that matches the tracks the boots leave behind. This false form is chosen by the bearer and may be changed at any time. This form is completely undetectable to entities physically observing the bearer.

Conductive Gloves

While wearing these gloves, the bearer may expend a charge as part of any action to receive a bonus to its check equal to their full Potency. These gloves have a number of charges equal to half the bearer's Potency, all of which are restored after a Long Rest.

Dark Lantern

This onaian lantern projects a field of light only visible to the bearer. It illuminates everything within a 3 unit radius, allowing the bearer to see through illusions without dispelling them.

Dimensional Key

A relatively non-descript tablet of aurion capable of instantiating and sustaining an interdimensional matrix attuned to the caster. When activated, the tablet expands outward to reveal a yawning portal to a small 5x5 unit pocket dimension. The pocket dimension remains stable even when not open, allowing the bearer to store items in there for safe keeping. While attuned to a dreamer, no other dreamer can use the key to access the pocket dimension without authorization from the bearer.

Farseer Orb

Two nested, concentric rings which fuse together when not in use. When attuned but inactive, the orb acts as a ward: its bearer cannot be taken by surprise, as the orb will alert them to any incoming danger, and will even attempt to rouse its dreamer from sleep if necessary. When activated, the rings separate and begin to hover in the air before their wielder, orbiting their shared center point; the wielder may then direct the orb to a location in the distance, an image of which will be conjured with as much clarity and/or magnification as the user chooses.

Flowsight Goggles

A pair of spectacles with enchanted lenses. While inactive, the wearer has perfect vision and cannot suffer from visual Impairment. While active, the wielder's vision changes to perceive the flow of azai and azonian constructs directly: it affords them the ability to navigate the world regardless of darkness, barriers, etc., but washes out much specific details, like facial expressions, or text upon a sign.

Glamorous Items

Prized by performing artists throughout the Dream, these items, be they instrument or attire, allow the wielder to produce minor illusions in the local area around them as part of a performance.

Imperceptibility Cloak

A shimmering cloak which renders its bearer invisible when they choose to activate it. While inactive, the cloak will still affect othersâ€™ perception of the wearer: any attempt to observe them or their actions has a -1 d20 Polarity penalty.

Platform Boots

While attuned to these boots, the user may remove a single stack of Entangled or Immobilized at the start of their Faction Turn for free. If activated while falling, these boots will arrest any momentum, causing the wielder to hover in place.

Power Pack

Packs such as these serve as additional repositories of azai, capable of siphoning and storing excess energies from the bearer within them and dispensing it at a time of need. The bearer can activate this item to end the Recharge of a Skill or nullify the cost and risk of Free-Casting for a Round. Once used, this item has a Recharge of 10-12.

Preservation Charm

Dreamers attuned to this item will become stable and regain 1 Vitality if they begin their Faction's Turn as Unconscious. If they are in an Unstable/Mortal state at the start of their Faction's Turn, they revert to being Unconscious instead.

Sentry Totem

These items served as an early inspiration for a Luminary's Projectors. When activated, the Sentry Totem is capable of feeding information back directly to the user, allowing them to perceive the space the Totem is placed as though they were standing there, or even to produce holographic images of it via their unitool. While activated in this way, the Totem bonds to the surface it is affixed to, and cannot be moved or disfigured by conventional means.

Shaper's Tools

A pair of artisan's tools that come in the form of stylized gloves. One hand projects a field around an object, with a size determined by the bearer, while the other hand may be used to manipulate objects within the projected field of its counterpart. These are used in the forging and shaping of all types of items, be it the sharpening of a blade, sculpting stone into a particular shape, or welding plates of an airship's hull together.

Shimmering Attire

Garments woven from onaian threads which have the ability to change their colors and patterning in response to the wearer's thoughts. This may be done as an Action, and can alter visual aspects of the garment, but not any of its physical dimensions.

Static-Charged Cloak

While attuned to this cape, the wielder can activate it in order to make it assume a rigid form. These were initially crafted for use as gliders and makeshift partitions, but are also popular in cultures that prize broad shoulders.

Talisman of Desire

While attuned to this item, a dreamer receives a +1 d20 Polarity bonus to all social checks. When activated, it produces a Sirensong.

Yujai Tablet

A talisman invested with the spirit of an ancestor. Such talismans are exceedingly rare, and every one is unique, based on the capabilities of the invested spirit and the conditions of their binding.

Sigils

Sigils invest dreamers with a magical enhancement which scales with their Spell Tier. Unlike talismans, which are generally used to provide additional utility not normally available to a dreamer, sigils are primarily employed to enhance the natural talents of the dreamer they are inscribed upon. Sigil enchantments of the same type do not stack: even if a dreamer has multiple sigils with the same enchantment, they will only benefit from a single instance of the enchantment; this has no effect on a dreamer with multiple unique sigil enchantments.

Movement Speed Bonus

Dreamers bearing a sigil with this enchantment gain an additional 2 units of Movement speed per Spell Tier.

Secondary Stat Bonus

Sigils invested with this type of enchantment are tied to a specific spell college of the dreamer's choosing. The sigil provides a static bonus to checks involving the selected college equal to the dreamer's Spell Tier. A dreamer can have multiple instances of this type of sigil enchantment active so long as each sigil confers a bonus to a different Collegiate Training stat.

Free-Casting Bonus

This sigil is capable of tempering the azai it accumulates, rendering it much easier to manage and craft architecture with. Once it has accumulated enough azai, the dreamer may unleash this energy for a static bonus equal to their Spell Tier on any free-cast spell; actions empowered in this way cannot trigger a roll on the Vortex Table regardless of their Degree of Success. Once used, this feature has a Recharge of 12 as the sigil begins accreting and tempering more azai; the Recharge threshold decreases by 1 per Spell Tier (e.g. 11-12 at tier 2, 10-12 at tier 3, etc.).

Damage Reduction

This sigil fortifies a dreamer's physical and astral bodies. All incoming damage from any source is reduced by an amount equal to their Spell Tier.

Potency Boost

Sigils infused with amplification architecture are capable of increasing a dreamer's base Potency by 2 per Spell Tier.

Acquiring Items

Items can come from anywhere the GM and PCs decide they might most reasonably be found. They may also be crafted readily by either PC or NPC artisans, typically those who have trained in Enchantment magic.

Organizations

Many to most of the items within the game can be crafted by artisans who generally operate as members of crafting Guilds. Some are specific to a single Court, whereas others are spread throughout Thymora. Having a higher standing with these Guilds, or other organizations like them, will give you access to higher quality goods.

Organizations which operate across multiple Courts will typically carry similar inventories at their disparate locations, and will generally be happy to do business with characters at the level of clearance they have attained with the organization directly, regardless of their standing with the local Court. Likewise, having high standing with a local Court can do a lot to grant PCs access to guild inventories which they may not usually find themselves.

Standing

There are four levels of standing a dreamer may achieve relative to any organization they encounter: Friend, Member, Trusted, and Confidante. Because of the potential power and/or rarity of customized magical items, organizations are not always willing to part with their treasures for currency alone: for some vessels of power, a level of trust is also involved.
