Status Effects

Many, if not most, Skills in the game apply status effects as a component of their usage, typically as a result of a Plus. There are 21 unique status effects in the game, and every entity can suffer the consequences of two distinct effects at a time. If a new status effect is successfully applied to an entity already afflicted with two other effects, one of the old effects may be overwritten, or application of the new status effect may be sacrificed for a +2 Potency boost on their action. Characters will often select abilities based specifically on which status effects they will hopefully inflict.

Removing Effects

Every status effect in the game may be removed via a Counter action, and effects being maintained via an active Channel may sometimes also be mitigated by interrupting the channeling entity. Additionally, stacking effects lose a single stack of their effect when its penalty is applied to an action: a stack of Entangled is removed when an affected entity executes a Movement action, for instance.

Types of Effects

Generally, if a character has access to a Skill which allows them the ability to inflict a specific Status Effect, the dreamer is thought of as understanding how to achieve such an effect. They may reproduce such Status Effects as they have access to via Free-Casting or other actions as they see fit.

Projected
Description Stacking effect, max 2 stacks. Indicative of when an entity is astral projecting: one stack indicates that a target's consciousness is projected elsewhere, but they still retain a connection to their physical body which would allow them to feel physical sensations (such as someone trying to wake them up); two stacks indicates a target has been severed from their physical form and are unaware of anything happening to their body. Removing the first/final stack of Projected (where the character is still projecting but maintains awareness of their physical body) requires an action, but does not require a check; characters in this state may also have the effect removed if they are targeted by a hostile action, or if another entity actively attempts to rouse them. Characters suffering from two stacks of Projected must first succeed on a successful Counter check before being able to awaken, which costs an additional Action unless the Counter achieves a Plus Degree.
