Entity Basics

Entities are the operators within the game space whose actions the GM and PCs will be role-playing. While every entity has access to the same general actions, the bonuses they have towards succeeding in those actions will all be different based on several variables: their Statistics, Style, Attributes, Items, and their personal area of expertise denoted by their choice of Specialization. Players will have almost complete control over the customization of these variables.

Attunement

Dreamers, regardless of their parents' origins, undergo physical changes in accordance with their individual attunement as they reach maturity: a process which continues as they grow over the course of their lives. A character's Attunement affects not only how they appear, but also how their magic manifests: a Fireheart's spells might naturally take on a fiery quality unless purposefully altered, whereas an Airheart's energy may represent the lightning discharged in storm clouds.

Catalytic Attunement

The facet of Change, associated with fire, birth, death, and transmutation magic. Passionate, driven, and often self-motivated, Catalytic dreamers often have a cause and are ready to see it through. They prefer dynamic environments, but whether that's a leading role in the Legion, the smooth operation of an Optio, or the rush of gladiatorial combat is entirely up to the individual.

Catalytic dreamers have specific acumen with spells that deal with fire or the concentration and release of energy. Though the talent is often associated with a seeming immunity to fire, they are able to withstand and safely conduct large amounts of many types of energy through their body without being harmed.

Fluid Attunement

The facet of Flow, most commonly associated with azai, the interstellar Flow, and conduction and harmonic magics. Though they may not intend to, Fluid dreamers often tend to treat life as a grand adventure in which the journey, not the destination, is the primary focus. Though there is a perhaps earned stereotype for their affinity to coastal regions, they are just as likely to settle roots somewhere that brings them peace as they are to live a life of traveling.

Fluid dreamers have specific acumen with spells controlling matter in its liquid state. They have an innate ability to tread upon or traverse any liquid.

Luminal Attunement

The facet of Light: all occurrences leave behind a trace. Light, echoes, records, stories, augury magic, and the Reliquary are aligned with this attunement. Luminal dreamers are easily identified for the unique bioluminescent patterns on their skin, which often seem to shift and flow under close inspection. They make enthralling visual artists and lecturers, but are just as often happy to be hidden away in a quiet corner of an archive.

Luminal dreamers have a relationship with light and its presence and absence within the Dream. They have a bonus to spells interacting with these forces, and may use these powers to comfort and mesmerise others.

Organic Attunement

The facet of organized Growth. Associated with animist magics, the emergent miracles of life and consciousness, and the natural changes that occur in the dream as a result of this. Organic attuned dreamers live for cultivation, whether it be collecting trinkets, growing things, or helping others, and they typically exude an aura of calm. As a result, they are often regarded with easy trust; even, and sometimes especially, including wildlife.

Organic dreamers are especially proficient with spells involving life and other emergent processes. They have enhanced natural regeneration and the ability to use their telepathy to commune with all living beings rather than just dreamers.

Spatial Attunement

The facet of Space: the stage upon which the Dreamscape is set. Often associated with air, wide open space, the Interstice, and translocation magics. Visionary attuned dreamers often find themselves unbound by gravity, developing access to flight naturally as they age. Energetic and flighty, they feel most at home in wide open spaces, scenic vistas, and anywhere they've yet to explore.

Spatial dreamers are specifically in tune with the air and empty spaces of the dream, giving them proficiency in spells relating to these areas. They have an innate ability to fly, and can hold their breath for as long as they choose.

Static Attunement

The facet of Stasis. Most commonly associated with azon, occlusion magics, and the physical world. Static-attuned dreamers often have tough skin, but whether this manifests as a thick hide or as semi-fluid rock or metal or somewhere in between will vary from person to person. They often feel comfortable in enclosed spaces, or on or under solid ground, which, for many of them, proves as easy to move through as air.

Static dreamers have specific acumen with spells regarding solid matter and the physical world. They have the ability to move through unwarded walls and other solid surfaces.

Visionary Attunement

The facet of Possibility: things that have been or could come to pass. Associated with the Oracle, dreams, perception and enchantment magics. Natural shapeshifters, Visionary attuned dreamers are uniquely capable of experiencing the world from varied vantage points. Though most eventually sculpt a preferred appearance for themselves, it is also not unusual for visionaries to alter their visage in a manner akin to changing clothes.

Visionary dreamers are proficient with spells that augment an object's existing physical properties, and have the ability to shapeshift.

Specializations

While it is considered vital that dreamers come to an understanding of all nine colleges of azaian magic, it is known that all paths to Awakening eventually converge: through deep contemplation and study of a single facet of the azonai, one may come to a greater understanding of its whole. Once their general understanding and capabilities are relatively comprehensive, dreamers educated in Thymoran Academies focus their studies upon a single College to which they have the strongest affinity, and in so doing acquire a Specialization, or spec, which corresponds both to the college they studied and the cultural practices associated with it.

Every spec has a unique binary tree of ten talent nodes (corresponding to ten levels) which Players can traverse in any manner they choose. Characters receive the first talent in their tree when they select their Specialization, and may select a single talent to activate for every level they gain.SubHead

Alchemist

College of Transmutation Specialist

Like those of the Dreamheart attunement, Alchemists are able to change their form at will. Unlike other shapeshifters, however, Alchemists are unique in their ability to alter the fundamental properties of their being. They receive a passive bonus to their Transmutation Collegiate in addition to their training; when they change their form, the Alchemist is able to shift this passive bonus to any other Collegiate of their choosing, allowing for an incredible amount of versatility.

Arcanist

College of Conduction Specialist

Arcanists train their astral bodies rigorously, which increases the range of their spells and allows them to tap into currents of the Azaian Flow, empowering them to conduct amounts of azai beyond what other dreamers could ever conceive. With enough training, they are able to use their own bodies as a point of confluence for multiple currents, effectively doubling their Potency and Secondary stats while they are able to maintain the effect.

Conduit

College of Harmonics Specialist

Conduits have studied the regenerative cycles of the Dream and have learned the ways in which to function as a catalyst for the exchange of energies between the dimensions of the Oracle, the Dream, and the Reliquary. The energies they channel manifest in the surrounding environment as an Aura: a field of ambient azai that clings to them, creating an energizing effect on the Conduit and those near them.

Luminary

College of Perception Specialist

Luminaries specialize in the use of Perception Projectors: custom engineered devices capable of projecting illusory effects and minor amounts of force. Combining their tools with a study of techniques that alter an entity's memory and sensory perception makes Luminaries peerless artists and infiltrators.

Operative

College of Enchantment Specialist

Operatives are masters of technical magic, responsible for the design and crafting of most magical devices and arcanomatons. Every Operative has made custom modifications to their unitool, and while many of their changes will be unique, they all are sure to include capabilities for the production of a Swarm: imperceptibly small wisp constructs of pure magic that permeate the area within the Operative's spell range, and may even power their devices, such as mobile Turrets.

Phantom

College of Translocation Specialist

Phantoms learn how to warp the fabric of the dream, allowing them to move (themselves and others) through space easier than other dreamers. They are able to trigger additional actions as part of their Plus results by bending space around them such that they act faster than others could ever hope to.

Sage

College of Augury Specialist

The Sage has, through study and meditation, begun to catch glimpses into the way the Dream may unfold before them, affording them the ability to react to events before they happen. Drawing further on these talents allows them to help guide the course of present events by either foiling or enhancing the actions of other entities at the very moment they act.

Sentinel

College of Occlusion Specialist

Sentinels bear the markings of their training upon their body in the form of glowing Runes: self-conjured azonian sigils which allow them to store pockets of azaian energies within themselves to be unleashed in tandem with their actions. Using these techniques, a Sentinel can turn themselves into a magical dynamo, unleashing surging forces as easily as creating unassailable barriers to lock down entire areas.

Spiritist

College of Animism Specialist

Through their study of the natural world and the forces that bind the disparate phenomena of the Dream together, Spiritists form a strong bond to the spiritsong and an affinity with all living entities. Every Spiritist eventually finds companionship in an Avatar: an entity with whom they have soul bonded through loyalty, love, and magic. The pair operate as two bodies sharing a mind, amplifying each others' strength and that of those around them.

Style

Even though everyone has a focus, they are not all employed identically. Similar to handwriting, everyone has their own way of crafting spells; this distinction is referred to as one’s Style.

A character's choice of style will govern the ways in which they employ magic: the Potency of their spells, the range at which they are effective, the status effects they can inflict, and unique traits and abilities.

Echomind Style

A fast-paced mid-range style that focuses on approaching solutions from multiple avenues.

Unique among their stylistic counterparts, Echominds duplicate every check they attempt that involves their Potency. Their base Potency is lower than practitioners of other styles to account for this, as they favor speed over power in their executions: by acting quickly, an Echomind creates more opportunities for themselves to succeed.

Farseer Style

A unique style that involves wielding one's focus with their astral limbs, allowing them to act from two positions simultaneously.

While most dreamers require their focus close to hand in order to channel azai through it, Farseers are not so restricted. Almost as though it has a mind of its own, their focus is able to move independently of its wielder, effectively granting them an extra vantage point from which to see and act as though they were there themselves.

Hollowform Style

A versatile mid-range style that focuses on patient, fluid techniques.

Just as a liquid adapts its shape and size to the vessel that carries it, so too do Hollowform practitioners seek to adapt to any situation. Patient and mindful, they flow around problems rather than facing them head on. Just as a lever may allow an entity to move an object far greater than their strength would normally allow, the subtle application of effort dispensed in the appropriate manner can alter the course of even the strongest currents.

Legionnaire Style

A close range, methodical style centered around precise, high Potency executions that build upon each other.

When it comes to taking a stand, Legionnaires are first on their feet. True heavyweights, they are often viewed as protectors brave enough to stand for themselves and others. Their brawny physicality reinforces a stereotype of shooting first and asking questions later, which most of them embrace: for the shrewd Legionnaire, nothing is so fortuitous as being underestimated.

Spellmark Style

A long range style focused around careful observation and calculated applications of force.

Archetypal sharpshooters, Spellmarks are both technical and patient. By training their magics at extreme ranges, they prioritize clear vantage points and clarity of information to properly contextualize their decisions. To a Spellmark, careful and cogent prep time is synonymous with a flawless execution: consider twice, act once.

Potency

A character's base Potency is derived from their choice of Style. Every other ability they use or spell they cast is subject to, and scales with, their Potency. Potency values apply fully on a Full Success, and are halved on a Partial Success; for this reason, base Potency values are always even numbers.

Attributes

Unlike Statistics, a character's Attributes confer bonuses that are generally not numerical. Most of them are very open-ended, as their purpose is primarily to provide mechanics which confirm the story a Player is trying to roleplay with a character.

Touchstone

This is the fundamental idea that either defines or drives a character. Whether this is something they consciously know, or if it is simply something to inform their PC’s own understanding of them is an individual determination each PC can make for themself. This is not a choice that is restricted to a finite list, and is not tied to any statistical benefits for the character. However, when used creatively, they can be used to give PCs an edge when invoked via a Lucidity token.

Dalyny’s Player determines she is a very physically active individual who, due to her minute stature, would never be able to be the strongest or fastest of her peers. Instead, she has sought to become the most capable, and has spent years training as a performing artist with members of her community. Her dynamic training has developed strength through excessive ranges of motion, rendering Dalyny capable of feats many others lack the core strength, coordination, or are simply too big to accomplish, and is especially versed in being vaulted into the air by a strong partner. With a solid idea for their character in mind, Dalyny’s Player chooses Acrobat as her Touchstone.

Quirk

As with a character's Touchstone, their quirk is an open-ended, non-statistical determination, but they should be something that can cause complications for the character in the right circumstances, and afford them unique insight in others. These aren't meant to be debilitating, but to further contextualize the character in the minds of both their Player and the rest of the group. A Quirk like “Social Smoker” could definitely be a problem if they’re planning for some serious physical exertion, but in others it could be a definitive benefit for easing social tension.

Because much of Dalyny's training involved coordinated, choreographed performances with a team, most of her goals for training have been derived from the needs of a group: if a performance involved fitting into smaller spaces, she might train contortion; if it involved being vaulted through the air, she might practice landing smoothly from a fall. Even though she would pursue her goals with a single-minded determination once she had one, she always had trouble picking a direction of her own. Dalyny's Player chooses Team Reliant as her Quirk to represent her strong preference for interdependence within a group to individual self-sufficiency.

Traits

A character's traits are similar to their Touchstone and Quirk, in that they are beyond the scope of choosing from a list of options. Unlike a character's Touchstone or Quirk, which serve to illuminate an inner truth about how a character experiences the world, their Traits are meant to symbolize their ties to it. More specifically (and practically), this will allow Players to begin to tie their character's stories both to the game space and each other.

Traits are generated as part of a group activity during character creation as a way for Players to begin to intertwine the stories of their PCs within the game space prior to play.

Kelorin and Alfred love engaging in trickery, especially in regards to members of the court who meander through the gardens, or anywhere that Alfred can fly and spy upon people in an inconspicuous manner. Depending on the target, one of the pair will cause a distraction or keep a look out while the other does the deed. They've come up with a series of different maneuvers they will employ to collect trinkets from unsuspecting passersby. Thus, they choose Petty Thieves for their first Trait.

Dalyny's player is handed Kelorin's paper after they swap, and immediately decides that the two would be familiar: not because they have a relationship, but simply because they would have seen each other around the raven's aery. The player decides Dalyny loves animals, but especially birds because of how graceful they look when they fly; it's one of the reasons she loves her acrobatic practices. As a way to get some of the ravens to trust her, Dalyny would come to practice by herself near some of the feeding areas for the birds. They seemed skittish when she would move too quickly, though, so she spent most of her time trying to achieve static holds in certain poses. If she stood still long enough, Dalyny discovered some of the birds would come perch on her limbs...and so she immediately began training to balance on a single hand or foot to provide as many perches as possible for her feathered friends. They write Statuesque for Dalyny's second Trait to signify her ability to stand still long enough as to appear solid.

Suul's player reviews the paper and decides that, unlike Kelorin or Dalyny, Suul might seek out crowded spaces for relaxation. The ability to form connections via simple social rituals are comforting to Suul, especially when it comes to smoking: the tendency for people to congregate and discuss things while smoking is an activity Suul enjoys and finds it easy to learn interesting things about people while doing it. They choose Social Smoker for Suul's third Trait.

Invoking Attributes

During play, Players will invariably come upon situations where their attributes come into play. In these instances, a Player may spend a Lucidity Token to automatically receive a Full Success degree on a related check, or receive a situational bonus as determined by the GM.

Changing Traits

Attributes are meant to expose only *some* of the facets of a character, and sometimes it can be best to focus on others. They can be changed at any time, so long as all Players involved are on the same page with the terms of the change.

Statistics

All characters start with a base 6 Vitality, 0 in all other statistics, and 5 points to invest as they choose into either primary stats or collegiate training. Each level after the first will grant a character 2 additional points to invest as they see fit to reflect the growth they’ve achieved during their journey. Investment into primary stats will increase a character’s acumen: they will become more potent, intelligent, and intuitive. Training in collegiates reflects time taken to learn new skills, and increases the number of tools at a character’s disposal via new spell architectures and free-casting bonuses.

Secondary Stats derive their base value from their associated Primary statistic. Except Vitality, each Primary statistic has two associated secondary stats: one which affects their physical capabilities, and one which affects their magic. Items, enchantments, and other effects will generally provide increases to Secondary statistics rather than Primary stats, however, so players may expect to see them with higher bonuses than their Primary counterparts.

Primary Stats

Vitality

A relatively straightforward statistic. A character's max Vitality is equal to their Vitality stat value. Unlike other Primary statistics, Vitality has no Secondary stats associated with it and increases by 1 per level regardless of stat investment.

Cognition

Associated Secondary Stats: Recovery, Adaptation

A character's mental acumen. A marker of how well they employ reason and assimilate knowledge.

Force

Associated Secondary Stats: Attack, Projection

A character's physical prowess. A representation of how in tune they are to their own physical movements, and their ability to affect the Dream around them.

Insight

Associated Secondary Stats: Defense, Unification

A character's ability to perceive the world around them, intuit the actions of others, and synergize the actions of their mind and body.

Collegiate Training

There are nine colleges of magic, all of which can be studied regardless of specialization. There are a finite amount of training points a character can invest in any college relative to their own advancement: each training bonus caps at 4, and each point allocated beyond the first requires a character to have attained a certain level before they may be trained.

Training in a college gives a character access to their d6 pool to augment affected checks, as well as knowledge of 2 spell architectures from within that college per point of investment. By default, a character is granted free training in the college relating to their chosen class, conferred automatically upon meeting the level threshold requirements for the training.

Items and Equipment

There are four different types of equipment that characters will be concerned with: their focus, their unitool, talismans, and arcanetics.

Every character will begin with at least a focus and unitool in their possession, as these are considered central to life within the Dream by society at large and provided to all free of charge.

Focus

Characters have a single focus which serves to facilitate their spellcasting, similar to how you may expect a wizard in other settings to always have a wand or staff. Most dreamers acquire their foci at a young age, before entering the School of Style, and it is usual for them to keep the same one for their whole life. The aurion cores of foci grow in power in tandem with their wielder, to the point where most dreamers view their focus as an extension of themselves.

Unitool

After a dreamer's focus, nothing is so indispensable to them as their universal tool. Traditionally worn or implanted as a bracelet on either wrist, it has several standard functions: it acts as a communication device, allowing telepathic communication between users across vast distances; it acts as an information retrieval and storage device, capable of projecting holographic displays of data; it allows the user to interact with all magical technologies; it modulates a dreamer's reserves of azai, creating a protective Shield around them; it can store and implement certain spell architectures, even if the user has not yet learned the spell themselves.

Talismans and Arcanetics

Talismans and Sigils are similar in function, but not necessarily in form. Talismans are freely traded magical items which are rendered inert when not attuned to a specific dreamer. Arcanetics, however, is a catchall term for implanted sigils and enchanted prosthetic limbs which cannot be deactivated or removed once endowed.

While dreamers may have as many talismans as they wish to accumulate and carry, they are limited in how many active talismans and/or arcanetics they may benefit from simultaneously, with a cap equal to their current Spell Tier.

Acquiring New Items

Talismans are plentiful throughout the Dream and can be found in many places, either out on adventures or in the shops of Courts and Guilds that Players may interact with. Arcanetic implantation typically requires a specialist, but they are likewise popular amongst the various organizations of the Dream. If PCs are so inclined, they may also train to become artisans themselves.
